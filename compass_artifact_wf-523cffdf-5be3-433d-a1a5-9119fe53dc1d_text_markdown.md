# Beyond QuIP#: A Literature Review of LLM Post-Training Quantization Advances (2024–2026)

## TL;DR
- **QuIP#'s core ideas were extended in two directions:** its own Cornell group replaced vector quantization with **trellis-coded quantization (QTIP, NeurIPS 2024)** to break the exponential codebook barrier, and a wave of **rotation-based methods (QuaRot, SpinQuant, DuQuant, FlatQuant)** carried the "rotate to suppress outliers" idea into activations and KV-cache for full W4A4 inference. As of 2026, QTIP-class trellis methods and newer Leech-lattice VQ are the SOTA for 2-bit weight-only PTQ.
- **The simple "go lower is better" narrative did NOT survive cleanly.** QuIP#'s prediction that 2-bit would scale better than 3-bit was partly vindicated for PTQ, but "Scaling Laws for Precision" (Kumar et al., 2024) showed overtrained models degrade *more* under PTQ, and ParetoQ (Meta, 2025) found 2–3 bit (via QAT) — not 1-bit or 4-bit — sits on the accuracy/size Pareto frontier. Native low-bit training (BitNet b1.58) is a separate QAT track, not PTQ.
- **Extreme PTQ of Turkish LLMs is a genuine, largely unexplored thesis gap** as of early 2026 — but the general "calibration-language matters" idea is now established prior work, so the novelty must be framed as the Turkish/agglutinative-specific case on Turkish-native benchmarks.

## Key Findings

1. **Direct successor — QTIP (trellis-coded quantization)** solves the exact limitation that capped QuIP# at the 8-dimensional E8 lattice: vector quantization needs a codebook exponential in dimension, so QuIP# could not move to higher-dimensional lattices like Leech (24-D). QTIP uses a *stateful* trellis decoder that decouples codebook size from dimension, reaching effective dimensions >100 with near-optimal distortion. On Llama-2-7B 2-bit it cuts Wikitext2 perplexity to 5.91 vs QuIP#'s 6.19.
2. **Rotation lineage targets a different outlier than QuIP#.** QuIP# rotated *weights* to make them incoherent for better weight quantization. QuaRot/SpinQuant/DuQuant/FlatQuant rotate to suppress *activation* (and KV-cache) outliers, enabling W4A4 (4-bit weights AND activations) inference, which QuIP# never addressed.
3. **AQLM lineage advanced codebook VQ** (AQLM, ICML 2024) and fixed its fine-tuning (PV-Tuning, NeurIPS 2024 oral); VPTQ (Microsoft, EMNLP 2024) brought second-order optimization to VQ.
4. **The bit-width debate matured into a nuanced consensus**: 4-bit weight-only PTQ is nearly lossless and the safe default; 2–3 bit is viable only with VQ/trellis methods plus fine-tuning; sub-2-bit reliably needs QAT.
5. **Multilingual quantization research is active but Turkish-specific PTQ is essentially absent.**

## Details

### Theme 1 — Direct successors to QuIP# (Cornell RelaxML)

**QTIP: Quantization with Trellises and Incoherence Processing.** Albert Tseng, Qingyao Sun, David Hou, Christopher De Sa. NeurIPS 2024 (spotlight). arXiv:2406.11235.
- *Core idea:* QuIP# kept QuIP's incoherence processing (the Random Hadamard Transform makes weights approximately i.i.d. Gaussian) and added the E8-lattice "E8P" codebook. But vector quantization (VQ) requires a codebook with size exponential in the vector dimension d, so QuIP# was stuck at d≤8 (the E8 lattice, which achieves optimal 8-D unit-ball packing). Higher-dimensional lattices like Leech (24-D) would shape the Gaussian source better but the codebook becomes intractable.
- QTIP replaces VQ with **trellis-coded quantization (TCQ)**. A trellis is a directed graph; quantization is finding the minimum-distortion walk through it. The crucial property is that a *stateful decoder separates codebook size from the bitrate and effective dimension* — so you get the benefits of ultra-high-dimensional quantization (>100) with cost only *linear* in dimension. QTIP introduces a hardware-friendly "bitshift trellis" and compute-based (lookup-free) Gaussian codes that decode in as few as 2–4 instructions per weight, so matrix-vector multiply runs at >80% of peak memory bandwidth.
- *Results:* On Llama-2-7B at 2-bit, QTIP reaches Wikitext2 perplexity 5.91 vs QuIP#'s 6.19, and 3.78 vs 3.91 on Llama-2-70B (Wikitext2, ctx 4096). It is the SOTA combination of weight-only quality and fast inference, and is >3× faster than FP16.
- *Relation to QuIP#:* Same group, same incoherence-processing backbone, but TCQ removes the dimensionality ceiling that VQ imposed.

**Follow-on from the same group — YAQA / Model-Preserving Adaptive Rounding.** Albert Tseng, Zhaofeng Sun, Christopher De Sa. 2025. arXiv:2505.22988.
- *Core idea:* Most quantizers (including GPTQ/LDLQ, the rounding engine inside QuIP#/QTIP) minimize *per-layer* activation error as a proxy, ignoring how errors propagate to the network output. YAQA ("Yet Another Quantization Algorithm") is an adaptive-rounding algorithm that directly targets *end-to-end output* (KL-divergence to the original model), using a Kronecker-factored approximation of the full Hessian with near-optimal sketches. It gives the first end-to-end error bounds, is provably better than GPTQ/LDLQ, and empirically cuts error ~30%, even beating QAT — with no inference overhead. Its codebase builds directly on QTIP. (Note: FastKron and related 2025 follow-ups already propose Lanczos-based speedups to YAQA's Hessian estimation, so this sub-area is actively moving.)

### Theme 2 — Rotation / incoherence methods extended to activations & KV-cache

The key conceptual distinction for you: **QuIP# rotates weights to suppress *weight* incoherence/outliers for weight-only quantization. The methods below rotate to suppress *activation* (and KV-cache) outliers, which is what blocks low-bit *activation* quantization (W4A4).** This matters because activations have far worse outliers than weights, and W4A4 lets the matmuls themselves run in INT4 (compute savings), whereas weight-only quantization only saves memory bandwidth.

**QuaRot: Outlier-Free 4-Bit Inference in Rotated LLMs.** Saleh Ashkboos et al. (ETH Zurich, with Hensman/Microsoft). NeurIPS 2024. arXiv:2404.00456.
- *Core idea:* Uses *computational invariance* — multiplying weights by a Hadamard rotation H and activations by Hᵀ leaves the output unchanged (y = Wx = (WHᵀ)(Hx)). Rotation "spreads out" activation outliers across channels (the toy example: rotating [1,10] by 45° gives [7.78, 6.36], outlier gone). Inserts four rotations R1–R4; R1/R2 are fused offline into weights, R3/R4 are online (R3 for KV-cache, R4 for activations). Enables **W4A4K4** (weights, activations, KV-cache all 4-bit) on models up to Llama-2-70B: the 4-bit Llama-2-70B model "has losses of at most 0.47 WikiText-2 perplexity and retains 99% of the zero-shot performance"; 6/8-bit is lossless with simple round-to-nearest.
- *Relation to QuIP#:* Same Hadamard-incoherence principle, but applied to enable activation + KV-cache quantization rather than just weights.

**SpinQuant: LLM Quantization with Learned Rotations.** Zechun Liu et al. (Meta). ICLR 2025. arXiv:2405.16406.
- *Core idea:* QuaRot uses *random/fixed* Hadamard rotations; SpinQuant observes that different random rotations differ by up to 13 points in downstream accuracy, so it *learns* the rotation matrices R1/R2 on a small calibration set via Cayley optimization on the Stiefel manifold (keeping them orthonormal), while keeping R3/R4 as online Hadamard. It "narrows the accuracy gap [of W4A4KV4] with full precision to merely 2.9 points on the LLaMA-2 7B model, surpassing LLM-QAT by 19.1 points and SmoothQuant by 25.0 points," and for "LLaMA-3 8B models that are hard to quantize, SpinQuant reduces the gap to full precision by up to 45.1%" relative to QuaRot.
- *Relation to QuIP#/QuaRot:* Turns the fixed rotation into a *learned* one optimized for the quantization loss.

**DuQuant: Distributing Outliers via Dual Transformation.** Haokun Lin et al. NeurIPS 2024 (oral, top ~1%). arXiv:2406.01721.
- *Core idea:* Distinguishes **Normal Outliers** (consistently large channels) from **Massive Outliers** (extremely large values, which they localize to the FFN down-projection input). Hadamard rotation alone struggles with massive outliers. DuQuant builds a rotation matrix using outlier-dimension prior knowledge to redistribute outliers to adjacent channels block-wise, then adds a **zigzag permutation** to balance outliers across blocks, then a second rotation. Outperforms QuaRot at W4A4 and avoids the costly GPTQ step. A "DuQuant++" for MXFP4 was released in 2026.

**FlatQuant: Flatness Matters for LLM Quantization.** Yuxuan Sun et al. ICML 2025. arXiv:2410.09426.
- *Core idea:* Observes that even after Hadamard rotation, weights/activations can still be "steep and dispersed." Learns an *optimal affine transformation per linear layer* (not a single global Hadamard), made cheap via Kronecker-product decomposition into two small matrices fused into one kernel. Achieves <1% accuracy drop for W4A4 on Llama-3-70B, beating SpinQuant by 7.5%, with up to 2.3× prefill and 1.7× decode speedup. Current SOTA for W4A4 as of mid-2025.

### Theme 3 — AQLM lineage and other vector-quantization advances

**AQLM: Extreme Compression via Additive Quantization.** Egiazarian, Panferov, Kuznedelev, Frantar, Babenko, Alistarh. ICML 2024. arXiv:2401.06118.
- *Core idea:* Multi-Codebook Quantization — represents weights as a sum of codewords from several learned codebooks, with the objective reformulated to minimize layer *output* error (not just weight error), plus beam search for codes and block-level fine-tuning. First scheme Pareto-optimal below 3 bits/param. QuIP# and AQLM were the two main 2-bit VQ competitors before QTIP.

**PV-Tuning: Beyond Straight-Through Estimation for Extreme LLM Compression.** Malinovskii, Mazur, Ilin, Kuznedelev, Burlachenko, Yi, Alistarh, Richtárik. NeurIPS 2024 (oral). arXiv:2405.14852.
- *Core idea:* Both QuIP# and AQLM fine-tune compressed weights using the **straight-through estimator (STE)**, which the authors show is sub-optimal and poorly understood for discrete weights. PV-Tuning is a representation-agnostic framework that alternates between updating continuous parameters ("P" step) and the discrete assignments ("V" step), with convergence guarantees in restricted cases. Achieves the **first Pareto-optimal 2-bit quantization for Llama-2** — a 2-bit 13B model can beat any 7B quantization and rivals the FP16 7B.
- *Relation to QuIP#:* A drop-in improvement to the fine-tuning stage that QuIP#/AQLM both relied on.

**VPTQ: Extreme Low-bit Vector Post-Training Quantization.** Yifei Liu, Jicheng Wen et al. (Microsoft). EMNLP 2024. arXiv:2409.17066.
- *Core idea:* Uses second-order (Hessian-guided) optimization to formulate the VQ problem, channel-independent second-order refinement, and a cheap codebook initialization, plus residual/outlier quantization. At 2-bit it beats prior SOTA on Llama-2 (perplexity −0.01 to −0.34), Mistral, and especially Llama-3 (−4.41 to −7.34 perplexity), uses only ~10–18% of the quantization time, and gives 1.6–1.8× higher inference throughput. Can push 70B/405B to 1–2 bits.

**Newest VQ frontier (2025–2026):** GLVQ (Grouped Lattice Vector Quantizers, arXiv:2510.20984) and LLVQ (Leech Lattice Vector Quantization, arXiv:2603.11021) now use 24-D Leech-lattice codebooks and report beating QTIP and QuIP# at 2-bit — e.g., "under the 2-bit quantization regime on Llama 2-70B, GLVQ-32D achieves a perplexity of 3.36 on Wikitext-2, significantly lower than QTIP (3.78) and QuIP# (3.91)." This directly realizes the "go to higher-dimensional lattices than E8" ambition that motivated QTIP. (Both are recent preprints — treat as provisional, see Caveats.)

### Theme 4 — The "lower bit is better" scaling debate

**Did QuIP#'s prediction hold?** QuIP# (v1, Feb 2024) claimed it was "the first PTQ method to achieve superior scaling at 3 bits over 4 bits and similar scaling at 2 bits to higher bitrates," and predicted "in the near future, 2 bit models are likely to scale better than 3 bit ones." Verdict: **partially vindicated, but with major caveats.**

**Scaling Laws for Precision.** Tanishq Kumar et al. (incl. Aditi Raghunathan). ICLR 2025. arXiv:2411.04330.
- *Key finding that complicates "go lower":* **Overtrained models are MORE sensitive to post-training quantization.** Degradation from PTQ grows with the amount of pretraining data — to the point that, for a fixed inference precision, *more pretraining data can become actively harmful*. They unify PTQ, QAT, and low-precision training into one functional form where low precision reduces the model's "effective parameter count" (M_eff ≈ M(1 − e^(−P/γ))). This means modern heavily-overtrained models (Llama-3, trained on 15T+ tokens) are *harder* to quantize than older models like Llama-2 — exactly what VPTQ observed empirically (Llama-3 degrades far more than Llama-2 at 2-bit). Ouyang et al. (2024) reached a complementary conclusion: undertrained/larger models are more robust to low-bit quantization.

**ParetoQ: Improving Scaling Laws in Extremely Low-bit LLM Quantization.** Zechun Liu et al. (Meta). NeurIPS 2025. arXiv:2502.02631.
- *Key finding:* First unified framework comparing 1, 1.58, 2, 3, 4-bit on an apples-to-apples basis (this is QAT, not PTQ). Finds **ternary (1.58), 2-bit, and 3-bit lie on a comparable Pareto frontier and generally beat both 4-bit and 1-bit**; considering hardware, **2-bit offers the best accuracy-speed trade-off**. Their ParetoQ ternary 600M model reaches 58.7 accuracy, surpassing the prior "1-bit era" ternary 3B model with only 1/5 the parameters; and a 2-bit MobileLLM-1B beats a 4-bit MobileLLM-600M by 1.8 points — concrete evidence that, at a fixed memory budget, going lower-bit/bigger-model can win.
- *Net consensus as of 2026:* For **weight-only PTQ**, 4-bit is essentially free and the default; 2–3 bit is the active research frontier and is only competitive with VQ/trellis methods (QTIP, VPTQ, Leech-lattice VQ) plus fine-tuning. To go to 2-bit and *below* reliably, the field has shifted toward **QAT / native low-bit training**, not PTQ. The simple "just go lower with PTQ" narrative did not survive; "go lower, but with high-dimensional shaping + fine-tuning, and beware overtrained models" did.

**BitNet b1.58 (native low-bit training — QAT, NOT PTQ).** Ma et al. (Microsoft). arXiv:2402.17764; open 2B-4T model April 2025.
- *Core idea:* Instead of quantizing after training, train from scratch with ternary weights {−1, 0, +1} (log₂3 ≈ 1.58 bits) using a custom BitLinear layer (absmean weight quantization, 8-bit activations) and STE. Matrix multiplies become additions/subtractions — huge energy/latency wins. The open `bitnet-b1.58-2B-4T` (4T tokens) matches comparable FP LLMs at 2B scale with ~0.4 GB memory.
- *Why it's relevant but distinct:* It is the logical endpoint of "lower is better," but it sidesteps PTQ entirely — you cannot apply it to an existing pretrained model without retraining. This is the central reason the "go lower" story bifurcated: extreme bit-widths now belong to QAT, while PTQ's practical floor is ~2–3 bit.

### Theme 5 — Quantization for low-resource / non-English languages & calibration-data language

**How Does Quantization Affect Multilingual LLMs?** Marchisio et al. (Cohere). EMNLP 2024 Findings. arXiv:2407.03211.
- *Findings:* Quantization harms are far worse than English-only evaluation suggests. **Automatic metrics severely underestimate damage** — "a 1.7% average drop in Japanese across automatic tasks corresponds to a 16.0% drop reported by human evaluators on realistic prompts." **Non-Latin-script languages are hit hardest** (−1.9% vs −0.7% for Latin scripts on a 103B model), and challenging tasks (math reasoning, ~−13.1%) degrade fastest.

**Calibrating Beyond English: Language Diversity for Better Quantized Multilingual LLMs.** Chimoto, Elhoushi, Bassett (Lelapa AI / Cerebras / Wits). EACL 2026. arXiv:2601.18306.
- *Findings:* The single most overlapping paper to a Turkish-quantization thesis. It evaluates "eight calibration settings (five single-language and three multilingual mixes) on two quantizers (GPTQ, AWQ) on data from 10 languages." English-only calibration is sub-optimal: non-English and multilingual calibration sets cut perplexity, "with multilingual mixes achieving the largest overall reductions of up to 3.52 points in perplexity" (on Llama-3.1 8B and Qwen2.5 7B), and **language-matched calibration helps most for a specific target language**. Traces the effect to longer activation tails / better outlier coverage in multilingual calibration. GPTQ (Hessian-based) is more sensitive to calibration language than AWQ.
- *Counterpoint:* "English K_Quantization… Does Not Disproportionately Diminish Multilingual Performance" (arXiv:2503.03592) finds the opposite for llama.cpp K-quants — the field is *not settled*.

**Turkish-specific situation:** There is **no dedicated published paper applying PTQ to a Turkish-native LLM and evaluating the quality impact on Turkish-native benchmarks** (TR-MMLU, TurkishMMLU, etc.) as of early 2026. Turkish appears only as one language among many in multilingual studies (Marchisio et al.; the 55-language machine-translation study arXiv:2508.20893, which found GGUF variants most consistent and 2-bit degrading worst for low-resource/typologically-diverse languages) and is not isolated. Turkish-native models (Trendyol-LLM, ytu-ce-cosmos/Turkish-Llama-8b) have community GGUF quantizations (Q2_K–Q8_0) on Hugging Face but with **zero published quality evaluation** — one repo author explicitly asks users for feedback because none exists; the Kumru (VNGRS) team notably ships its 2B model "without even going through quantization." The closest methodological template is a Russian-language PTQ study (Springer, *Automatic Documentation and Mathematical Linguistics*, DOI 10.3103/S0005105525701389, quantizing 4–32B Russian LLMs to 2.0–4.25 bits, finding 4-bit highly robust), but Russian is fusional, not agglutinative — so the Turkish agglutinative angle remains distinct.

## Recommendations

**For understanding the field (staged reading):**
1. *Start with the direct successor:* Read **QTIP (2406.11235)** first — it is the natural "what comes after QuIP#" and explains exactly why E8/VQ couldn't scale and how trellis coding fixes it. Then **YAQA (2505.22988)** for the same group's latest rounding-algorithm advance.
2. *Then the activation-quantization branch:* **QuaRot → SpinQuant → DuQuant → FlatQuant**, in that order, to see the progression from fixed→learned→permutation→affine transforms. This is a distinct problem (W4A4) from QuIP#'s weight-only focus.
3. *Then the VQ/fine-tuning branch:* **AQLM → PV-Tuning → VPTQ**, then the 2025–26 Leech-lattice papers (GLVQ, LLVQ).
4. *Then the scaling debate:* **Scaling Laws for Precision (2411.04330) → ParetoQ (2502.02631) → BitNet b1.58 (2402.17764)**.

**For the Turkish thesis (this is a viable, defensible topic):**
- **Frame the contribution carefully.** Do NOT claim to be first to discover that calibration language matters — that is now established (arXiv:2601.18306, 2508.20893). DO claim: (i) first systematic extreme-PTQ (2/3/4-bit) study of Turkish-native LLMs, (ii) evaluated on Turkish-native benchmarks rather than translated MMLU, (iii) with explicit analysis of how agglutinative morphology / tokenizer fragmentation interacts with quantization error — an angle no existing paper covers.
- **Concrete experimental plan:** Take Turkish-native models (ytu-ce-cosmos/Turkish-Llama-8b, Trendyol-LLM, Kumru, or a Turkish-adapted Qwen like MODA), apply GPTQ/AWQ/QuIP#/QTIP at 2/3/4-bit, and compare English-only vs Turkish vs mixed calibration sets, evaluating on TR-MMLU/TurkishMMLU. Measure both automatic perplexity and a human or LLM-judge eval (because Marchisio showed automatic metrics hide damage).
- **Differentiate from prior work explicitly** in the related-work section: cite Marchisio (multilingual), Chimoto/EACL2026 (calibration language), the MT-55 study, and the Russian PTQ paper, then state precisely what each does NOT do for Turkish.
- **Verify the gap yourself** beyond arXiv: search YÖK Tez (Turkish thesis repository), ACL Anthology, and Turkish venues, since negative results are hard to prove from English-language search alone.

**Benchmarks/thresholds that would change the recommendation:** If you find Turkish *is* among the 10 languages in arXiv:2601.18306 with deep analysis, or if a Turkish PTQ paper appears in 2026, narrow your scope to the agglutinative-morphology mechanism (tokenization × quantization), which would still be novel.

## Caveats
- **Perplexity numbers are not cross-comparable across model families** (different tokenizers/vocabularies); the QTIP/QuIP#/GLVQ figures cited are within Llama families at stated context lengths and should be read as relative, not absolute.
- **Several "beats SOTA" claims (GLVQ, LLVQ, D2Quant, LiftQuant) are from recent arXiv preprints (late 2025–2026) that may not yet be peer-reviewed**; treat their leaderboard claims as provisional pending publication.
- **W4A4 (activation) results and weight-only PTQ results are not directly comparable** — they target different bottlenecks (compute vs memory bandwidth). Don't compare a QuaRot W4A4 number against a QTIP 2-bit weight-only number.
- **The multilingual-calibration literature contains a genuine contradiction** (calibration-language helps vs. doesn't), so any Turkish thesis should be prepared to find effects that are real but modest.
- The "no Turkish PTQ paper exists" conclusion is a negative result from search and cannot be proven exhaustively; the YÖK Tez / ACL Anthology checks above are essential before claiming the gap in print.
